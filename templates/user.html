Rohit Sharma - The Hitman of Indian Cricket

Celebrating the career of one of the greatest cricketers of modern times.

Early Life and Career

Rohit Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Maharashtra, is a cricketer who made his international debut in 2007. Known for his composure and technical prowess, Rohit's journey from domestic cricket to representing India is one of determination and resilience. Starting as a middle-order batsman, he found his place at the top of the order, becoming one of the most successful opening batsmen in world cricket.

Career Highlights

  • ODI Double Century: Rohit is the only player to score three double centuries in ODIs, with a career-high of 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014.
  • ICC T20 World Cup 2007: A key member of the Indian squad that won the inaugural T20 World Cup, marking the beginning of a stellar international career.
  • Record-Breaking Performances: Known for his big knocks, Rohit Sharma has hit five centuries in a single World Cup (2019) and countless match-winning innings.
  • IPL Success: As the captain of Mumbai Indians, he has led the franchise to five IPL titles, demonstrating leadership and tactical brilliance.

Playing Style and Strengths

Rohit Sharma is renowned for his effortless stroke play and ability to construct long innings. His cover drives, pull shots, and sweet timing make him a treat to watch. His adaptability across formats—ODIs, T20s, and Tests—has made him a key player for India, particularly in limited-overs cricket. His calmness under pressure and ability to play big knocks in crucial matches have earned him the moniker "The Hitman."

Personal Life

Rohit Sharma is not just a cricket star; he's also known for his humility and family values. He married Ritika Sajdeh in 2015, and together they have a daughter named Samaira. Off the field, he enjoys watching football, particularly his favorite team, Manchester City, and is involved in charitable activities.
